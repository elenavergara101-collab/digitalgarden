# Purpose
"To worship Otar, God of Blessings and Plenty"
(To trick people into worshiping Cyric, God of lies)
# Dogma
## Beliefs

1. [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Cyric\|Otar]] is a kind god who provides endlessly. He gives us challenges so that we might grow in our perfection.
2. He will not leave us, and in return we will never reject Him.
3. His domain and His people must remain perfectly pure.
4. Every waking moment should be an act of worship.
5. [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Cyric\|Otar]]’s language is holy, and must be kept as such.
6. Those under His care have been handpicked.
7. Other Gods are not to be praised and thus not to be mentioned.
8. Outsiders are impure. Extensive contact with them will defile you. 
	1. Animalistic and beastly creatures are even more impure, and extended contact will defile you in an unpardonable manner.
## Resulting Beliefs & Practices

1. Any challenge a person faces is God-given, this includes both circumstantial difficulties and individual troubles (mental disorders).
	1. These are to be overcome individually (it is taboo to talk about the struggles of others or even your own).
2. Rejecting [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Cyric\|Otar]] means leaving your family, friends, community. Everything.
	1. Members who do leave are often tracked down and re-enter the cult willingly OR are “convinced” by the Sacristans.
3. There is a very heavy emphasis placed on remaining pure.
	1. Topics of conversations are never to be heavy (death, difficulty, uncertainty, dislikes) unless done in the presence of a priest.
	2. Clothing must remain modest (in style & in monetary value) unless an event calls for it.
	3. Hair is not to be cut, dyed, or altered in any way.
	4. Lewd topics are only to be discussed with a clergy member present.
4. All everyday activities & hobbies typically are accompanied with prayer and any self-indulgent or indecent hobbies (dance, non-religious songs, dangerous physical exercise, etc.) are forbidden.
5. Common is taught but for most individuals it is not used outside of classes. However common must be spoken in the presence of an outsider.
6. While conversions are extremely rare, it became common if not encouraged to have many children.
	1. Having dozens of children is highly praised and can even raise your family’s status in the community. Families are large and people marry young.
7. Other gods aren’t discussed, the youngest generations don’t even know others exist.
8. The only outside contact occurs between high-ranking church officials & trusted outsiders. Prayers are performed immediately to cleanse the one who had the contact (3 day process) Most members fear outsiders.
{ #b806f6}

	1. Members are terrified of animalistic and beastly races (tabaxi, lionfolk, etc.), and often use them in fables to terrify their own children.

# Rites
## Ceremony of Life
- Occurs at the same time as the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Prayer of Mothers\|Prayer of Mothers]]
- The birth occurs in a designated room in the temple with 3 midwives (usually [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#^a209ab\|priest-wives]]).
- The umbilical cord is cut by the eldest of the midwives, the baby is bathed, and then is anointed with holy water.
- In an adjoining room the baby is placed on a small altar facing the chair where the father has been waiting. The father makes a choice:
	- Accept the child by holding them.
	- Reject the child (which only really occurs in cases of expected adultery) and leave the room. If this occurs the child will be abandoned. The mother will not be allowed to mourn.
- In the case of twins; the second child is considered a bad omen and is expected to live a life of servitude.
	- Both infants will be presented at the same time.
	- If the father rejects the second-born but accepts the first (which is not an uncommon practice) then the baby will be abandoned as normal. If he accepts both, the youngest is marked with a circle-cross on the nape of his neck, marking their destiny for servitude.
## Ceremony of Growth
- When a child reaches 90, they are now considered an adult.
	- The child (wearing light blue silk) will be escorted out of the temple by their father and announced as a son or daughter of Otar.
	- The child will publicly declare their faith, be anointed, and then they descend into the temple square to join the large celebration.
	- The party is often used to talk about potential engagements.
- If the child was a second-born twin, their ceremony will take place the day after their twins’.
	- They will wear grey and be escorted out by a High-Priest instead of their father.
	- They will offer themselves fully to the temple, declaring their desire to become a layman.
	- They get their own celebration, but the mood is typically a bit more somber.
	- They will experience the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Ascension\|Ascension rite]] the following day.

## Purification
- An impure individual is isolated for 3 days in a designated room (attached to the church but with a separate entrance).
- Bread, cheese, and water is provided but nothing else.
- The individual is led in prayer by a priest 5 times a day through a screen.
## Marriage
- The [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Purification\|Purification rite]] is given to both the bride & groom.
- The rite itself is a very public affair but the celebration (which occurs the next day) is very private.
- The [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#High Priests\|High-Priest]] will enter first, escorted by other male relatives and priests in plain white robes.
- The bride (wearing white and veiled) will follow, led by two married women from her family.
- The procession is entirely silent; meant to be an extension of the rite of purification the couple has just completed.
- The couple stands on a circular platform surrounded by the High-Priest and three other priests (who represent obedience, faith, and service).
- All vows are recited from memory and cannot be practiced during the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Purification\|Rite of Purification]].    
	- The bride promises obedience, stewardship, and to raise his children in their faith.
	- The groom vows protection, spiritual leadership, and honesty.
- The couple is anointed with oil.
	- The bride on her head & womb and groom on his head & right hand.
- A simple ring (never adorned) is placed on the bride.
- The newly married couple is taken to a sanctified chamber where the husband explains the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Consummation\|Consummation rite]].
	- This is often the first time they have ever been alone.
- The bride is expected to remain passive & agreeable, resistance is seen as impure and the bride may need to re-do the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Purification\|Rite of Purification]] before the marriage continues.
- The following day the bride & groom celebrate over breakfast with their own families before the father leads the bride to her new home.
## Consummation
- The couple is led into the room and the bride is undressed by her husband before he undresses himself.
- Two priests, through a thin screen, pray over the couple before they begin
- After the act the white cloth is collected to confirm the bride’s purity.
- If there is no blood the couple will repeat the rite again the following day.
- If there is again no blood she will be inspected for purity.
	- The marriage may be annulled and the wife may even [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Descension\|descend]] is she is found impure.
- The couple kneels for a final prayer led by the priests and then are dressed in simple grey robes and are led to separate chambers for the night.

## Prayer of Mothers
- Occurs simultaneously with the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Ceremony of Life\|Ceremony of Life]]
- Before the Rite begins the husband enters the adjoining room to wait for his child.
- As labor begins the mother is cloaked in a red veil & all other clothing is stripped from her.
- Once the baby is born the mother cannot look at it until her husband has finished the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#Ceremony of Life\|Ceremony of Life]].
- The mother is moved into a third chamber, where she will wait alone for a full hour before she learns of her husband’s decision.
	- If he accepts the child the mother is able to hold him briefly before he is sent to the nursery.
	- If he rejects the child she will remain wearing the red veil until her next period passes.
		- Often this time is spent entirely at home due to the shame associated with it.

## Ascension
#### Rite of Laymen
Requirements
- Unmarried
- Childless
- Seven days of absolute silence & obedience in the temple (mostly spent doing manual labor).
Rite
- The candidate’s head is shaved, they take a vow of silence (their tongue is branded with the circle-cross symbol), and a vow of chastity
- Dressed in rough grey cloth & tied with a white cord.
- They are then referred to only as “layman / laywoman”.
#### Rite of Priests
Requirement
- Must undergo the priesthood trial (3 days of fasting & dream rituals).
Rite
- The candidate is brought naked into one of the inner chambers.
- Two current priests cover his body in sigils of obedience & secrecy in a mixture of oil & ash.
- They kneel before the High-Priest overseeing them and justify their calling.
- A bronze ring is placed over their left-thumb, engraved with the symbol of Otar.
- They are then dressed & will later be announced at the next temple teaching.
#### Rite of High-Priests
Requirements
- Unmarried
- Childless
- Must have served as a priest for at least 9 years.
- Must have overseen 30 Marriages and 10 Rites of Death.
- Must undergo the High-Priesthood Trial (33 days of darkness, fasting, & dream rituals).
Rite
- The candidate is brought naked into one of the inner chambers.
- The Sacristan overseeing them will chant the rite.
- A silver ring replaces the bronze one on their left-thumb.
- They are then dressed & will later be announced at the next temple teaching.
#### Rite of Sacristans
Requirements
- Unmarried
- Childless
- Must have served as High Priest for 13 years.
- Must complete the Trial of Sacristans (9 days in a sensory deprivation chamber with only sacred texts and water).
Rite
- The Sacristan-elect is brought, naked (besides the new gold mask), to a hidden chamber.
- Intavar himself oversees the rite, granting the Sacristan Otar’s direct blessing and instructing them on how they should rule over their community.
## Descension
#### Requirements
- An individual must have fled the community / publicly renounced the teachings / committed a grave sin
- Approval must be granted by one of the Sacristans.
#### Rite
- The Rite begins at midday where the cult is gathered in the courtyard.
- The Sacristan who approved the descent will explain the sins the individual has committed and how their actions affected the community.
- The head of the household comes before the Sacristan carrying a cloth with the cult crest and a treasured item of the individual who left.
- Both are thrown into the a to symbol a breaking of ties from the cult (cloth) and family (belongings).
#### Soiling of the Family
- The entire immediate family kneels before the Sacristan.
- They each admit that they have been soiled by the individual's actions.
- The family may now not participate in Rites for 3 months.
- They may not speak the name of the individual or name a future child after them.
- The family typically deals with the social repercussions of the soiling for many years.

## Acknowledgment of Death
### Vigil of Stillness
- Occurs at sunset the day of death.
- The body is washed by two priest-wives (if female) or priests (if male) in water and oil.
- The body is wrapped in cloth & placed on a stone altar in a designated room of the temple.
- Immediate family will kneel and pray for three hours to rid the body of sin.
### Honoring & Burial
- Once deemed clean, the body will be wrapped in white, a small tablet with their name engraved will be placed on the body’s chest and buried. 
- The family speaks only of the deceased on the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#The Night of Echoes\|Night of Echoes]] once per year.
# Holidays
## The Night of Candles
The Night of Candles is celebrated every four months. It's a smaller holiday and is typically celebrated between 3-5 neighboring families. In the morning the families get together and each person makes a candle, the color corresponding to the virtue they want to work on the most. Then at dinner the candles are lit. 

The night of candles is a reminder that progression towards perfection must be continuous.
## The Night of Doors
The Night of Doors occurs only twice a year. Half of the families host open-house feasts while the remaining half go door-to-door visiting friends. During this holiday no doors are allowed to be locked. 

It is a reminder that openness and transparency are vital to keeping the community alive. 
### Event
Once, Ranadive and [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Therion Fen Zimou\|Therion]] tried using the holiday as an excuse to visit their father's study but were got in got into major trouble for it.
## The Night of Echoes
The Night of Echoes occurs in the temple only once per year. The entire community gathers for a lantern procession and then they gather to talk and tell stories about the dead. Foods that were loved by deceased family members are prepared & shared. 

The night is used to acknowledge the impact that sorrow and loss can have on us but also to acknowledge the beauty of life. 
## The Night of Splinters
The Night of Splinters is also celebrated once a year. The entire temple community community comes together and throws tokens of hardship into a large bonfire. Often times, people use a wooden board that they would write on (which is where the name of the holiday comes from). People who make public confessions while throwing their tokens are forgiven. Afterwards people eat roasted meats, cheese, and sweets. 

The night is a public reminder that we must be vulnerable to others.

## The Night of The Innocent
The Night of The Innocent is celebrated once a month. Children under 20 red robes with gold adornments & are given attention & sweets. There are dozens of activities they can participate in, including leaning about [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar#The Worshipers\|The Worshipers]]' professions. The older children are tasked with teaching them about the church & caring for thee young ones for the night. Pregnant women wear purple with gold adornments and are doted on by the men, other women, & elders.
# Daily Practices & Other Rituals
- Cult members are taught to was their mouth out with salt water each morning (typically right as they wake up). It serves as a reminder to speak honestly & faithfully.
- When entering and exiting a room (especially homes) members will briefly touch the doorframe & then their forehead and lips. It acts as a small blessing of safety and comfort.

# Subgroups
## The Worshipers
The Worshipers are a group of individuals who dedicate their lives to their crafts for [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Cyric\|Otar]]. They consist of many different branches:
- Music
- Dance
- Poetry
- Fine art
- Etc.
Because the work of a worshipper is so intense, almost everyone needs to retire when they get married or have their first child. The teachers of the worshippers are unmarried & childless so they can keep their focus on their work.

# Leadership
There are 5 ranks of leadership in the Church of Otar
## [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#The Blessed Intavar\|The Blessed Intavar]]
* Leader of the [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar\|Church of Otar]]
## Sacristans
* Second in command to [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#The Blessed Intavar\|The Blessed Intavar]]
* There are only three, one overseeing each of the Church's sites
* Final interpreters of the sacred law, advise high priests, and keep the forbidden holy texts
## High Priests
- They take direct orders from the Sacristans
- There are six total, two at each location
- Responsible for interpreting the divine will of [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava Character List#Cyric\|Otar's]] holy texts, overseeing major rites (or those that involve a priest), guiding and caring for the community as a whole

## Priests
- There are twenty-four total, so each high priest oversees four priests (eight per city)
- They handle the day-to-day religious tasks
	- Overseeing rites, making judgements, and managing names, records, and non-public holy texts
- The only leadership role that allows (and encourages) marriage and having children
	- Their wives (commonly known as priest-wives) are incredibly important in the community and often help with temple duties
{ #a209ab}

	- However many priests chose not to take wives in hopes they might become a high priest
## Laymen / Women{ #2af27a}

- Individuals who have fully dedicated their lives to Otar
- They often take vows of silence & make other offerings as a symbol of their dedication to service
- People who fail to get married (either due to failed engagement or they cannot find a partner) often enter the role to avoid shame
- Second-born twins are promised into the role of laymen / women
- They are responsible for all menial labor within the Temple, preparation for rites, and helping with any tasks requested of them by other members
# Logistics
The [[Rava Duskhorn/Rava The Church of Otar\|Church of Otar]] is a known, but extremely private group.
- Due to the cult’s beliefs on outsiders, the only contact the group has to the outside world is through high-ranking church officials
- They are very careful to maintain appearances

Most of their funding comes through its members. While money isn’t communal by any means a majority of income goes to the church who divides it as they see fit.

- The cult itself is largely self-sufficient, with need for only a few outside resources
- Members are able to submit goods to be traded in the outside world (most lay individuals make a majority of their income this way)
- The church taxes these goods
